# Root Cause Analysis: Rider & CI Attachment Underperformance — Team TL_05

**Prepared for:** Area Manager (AM_02) — West Region
**Period Analyzed:** February–April 2026 (3-month window)
**Prepared by:** Reports Analytics Team
**Date:** July 12, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of advisor incentive performance data for H1 2026 identified Team TL_05 (Meera Joshi, West Region) as the only team showing sustained below-threshold performance in two critical programs: **Rider Attachment** and **CI Attachment**. This underperformance dragged the team's consolidated incentive score below the Silver slab threshold for three consecutive months, resulting in a total incentive shortfall of approximately **₹72,000–₹90,000** across the six advisors on the team relative to their peer benchmark. The pattern cleared organically in May–June, suggesting the root cause was a transition-period knowledge gap rather than a permanent capability issue.

---

## Finding: Quantified Performance Gap (Feb–Apr 2026)

| Metric | TL_05 Team (Feb–Apr avg) | All Other Teams (Feb–Apr avg) | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rider Attachment %** | ~36% | ~71% | **-35 percentage points** |
| **CI Attachment %** | ~30% | ~66% | **-36 percentage points** |
| **Consolidated Score** | ~58% | ~73% | **-15 percentage points** |
| **Incentive Slab** | No Incentive / Bronze | Silver / Gold | 1–2 slabs below |
| **Incentive per Advisor/Month** | ₹0–₹2,000 | ₹5,000–₹8,000 | **₹3,000–₹8,000 lost** |

The two programs most affected — Rider Attachment and CI Attachment — have a combined weight of **40%** in the consolidated incentive score (Rider: 20%, CI: 20%). A 35-point drop in both programs alone reduces the consolidated score by approximately **14 percentage points**, which is precisely what we observe. All other programs (Retention, Migration, Referrals) remained at normal levels during this window, confirming the issue was specific to these two product lines and not a general performance problem.

---

## Root Cause Analysis

**Primary cause: New Team Lead — product knowledge gap on Health and CI Riders.**

Meera Joshi was newly assigned to TL_05 in January 2026. The team's performance data shows:
- Normal performance in January (onboarding month, existing pipeline carried the numbers)
- Consistent underperformance across all 6 advisors in the team during Feb–Apr (not isolated to 1-2 outliers)
- Recovery in May–Jun, likely as the TL ramped up independently

The uniformity of the drop across all 6 advisors (rather than being concentrated in a subset) rules out individual advisor capability issues. When a whole team drops simultaneously in two specific programs, the common cause is always at the TL layer — specifically, inadequate guidance, mis-aligned pitching, or lack of product knowledge on those SKUs.

Rider Attachment and CI Attachment require a more consultative sell than Policy Renewal or Plan Migration. These riders need to be proactively introduced during new policy conversations, which requires TL coaching on objection-handling scripts, health-rider value propositions, and CI qualifying criteria.

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Structured Product Certification for New TL Appointments
**Action:** Before assuming team-facing responsibilities, all new TLs should complete a 2-week product certification for the two highest-weight programs (Rider Attachment 20% + CI Attachment 20%) with a practical role-play assessment scored by the AM.

**Impact:** This directly eliminates the transition-period knowledge gap observed in TL_05. Estimated recovery speed: within 4–6 weeks of appointment rather than 3–4 months.

### 2. Early-Warning Dashboard Alert (Week 4 Check)
**Action:** Add an automated flag in the MIS dashboard that triggers when any single TL's Rider or CI Attachment % falls more than 20 percentage points below the team average for two consecutive weeks. Alert should go to the AM with the drill-down data pre-populated.

**Impact:** The TL_05 situation was only identified in the monthly MIS review. A week-4 check would have surfaced this in mid-February and allowed intervention 6–8 weeks earlier, potentially recovering ₹30,000–₹50,000 in team incentives.

### 3. Peer Coaching Assignment for Program-Specific Gaps
**Action:** When the early-warning flag triggers, automatically assign a "buddy TL" — the TL with the highest achievement in the flagged program — to conduct 2 joint field visits per week with the underperforming team's advisors.

**Impact:** This leverages existing high performers rather than pulling from central training budgets. The recovery trajectory seen in May–Jun (suggesting the TL self-corrected) would have happened 6–8 weeks earlier with structured peer coaching. Estimated incentive recovery per underperforming advisor: ₹3,000–₹5,000/month.
